📖 About This Intermediate Course

This intermediate course is designed for digital artists who have mastered the fundamentals and are ready to elevate their skills to a professional level. Through 7 comprehensive modules and over 50 portfolio projects, you'll learn advanced techniques used by industry professionals in concept art, illustration, and digital painting.

Each module builds systematically on previous knowledge, taking you from intermediate techniques to professional workflows. You'll create a diverse portfolio of work including character designs, environments, matte paintings, and stylized illustrations. By the end of this course, you'll have the skills and confidence to take on professional projects and develop your unique artistic style.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to complete the beginner course first? ▼
Yes, or you should have equivalent experience. This course assumes you're comfortable with Paintstorm's interface, basic brush usage, layers, color selection, and fundamental painting techniques. If you're brand new to digital art, start with the beginner course.
How long will it take to complete this course? ▼
The course contains approximately 40-50 hours of instruction and practice. However, the projects can take significantly longer depending on your pace and detail level. Most students complete the course in 3-6 months with consistent practice (5-10 hours per week).
Will this help me build a professional portfolio? ▼
Absolutely! Each module includes portfolio-quality projects. By the end of the course, you'll have completed 50+ pieces including character designs, environments, matte paintings, and styled illustrations. Module 6 specifically focuses on portfolio development and professional presentation.
What equipment do I need? ▼
A graphics tablet with pressure sensitivity is essential for this course. We recommend at least a mid-range tablet (Wacom Intuos, Huion, or XP-Pen equivalents). A computer capable of running Paintstorm Studio smoothly with multiple layers is also required.
Can I use these techniques in other software? ▼
Yes! While the course is taught using Paintstorm Studio, the concepts, techniques, and workflows are applicable to any digital painting software including Photoshop, Procreate, Krita, or Clip Studio Paint. The principles of lighting, composition, and color theory are universal.
