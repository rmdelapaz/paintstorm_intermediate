How dynamics shape expression

Brush dynamics connect your hand to the digital bristles. Imagine your stylus as a violin bow — pressure is force, tilt is timbre, and speed is tempo.

InputMap toUse
PressureSize, Opacity, FlowTapered lines, subtle shading
TiltAngle, RotationEdge shading, pencil-like flats
SpeedScatter, Texture DepthLively flicks, textured edges

Hands-on practice

Expressive line brush

Create a brush that sings with pressure and tilt. Use a small tip with a long taper and light texture.

<!-- Settings (escaped) -->
Brush Tip: Round (soft)
Spacing: 5–8%
Dynamics:
  - Size: Pressure (ease-in curve)
  - Opacity: Pressure (linear)
  - Angle: Tilt
Texture: Paper Fine (Depth 20%)
Stabilizer: Low–Medium

Textured painter

Use speed to vary scatter so quick strokes feel dry and lively, slow strokes lay smooth paint.

<!-- Settings (escaped) -->
Brush Tip: Custom oval
Dynamics:
  - Scatter: Speed (low)
  - Flow: Pressure (mid)
  - Rotation: Tilt
Texture: Canvas Medium (Depth 30%, Scale 90%)
Blending: Low smudge

Preset hygiene

Save often, tag clearly, and keep a small “favorites” set for speed. Think of it like a chef’s knife roll — only the essential tools travel.

<!-- Preset tips -->
Name format: dyn_line_soft_a, dyn_painter_textured_b
Tags: line, painterly, texture, smooth
Export set: Settings > Manage Resources > Export

Interactive — dynamics canvas

Click-drag to draw. Move faster to increase scatter; press longer to grow stroke size over time (simulating pressure).
